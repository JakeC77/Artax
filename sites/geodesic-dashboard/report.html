6. Recommendations

1. Implement Generic-First Formulary Design
Restructure formulary to prefer generics in high-volume classes (Diuretics, SSRIs, Corticosteroids). Target: Reduce brand ratio from 55% to 40% within 12 months.
2. Review Quantity Limit Policies
The 46.7% approval rate suggests QL criteria may be misaligned with clinical needs. Conduct clinical review and consider expanding limits for chronic maintenance medications.
3. Triptan Cost Management
With highest AWP ($274) and significant volume, implement step therapy requiring generic triptans (sumatriptan) before brand alternatives.
4. Diabetes Drug Class Optimization
DPP-4 Inhibitors show high volume (738 Rx). Evaluate GLP-1 vs DPP-4 clinical outcomes and consider value-based tier placement.
5. Establish Quarterly Monitoring
Implement dashboard tracking of brand/generic ratio, policy approval rates, and cost-per-Rx by drug class to measure intervention effectiveness.
